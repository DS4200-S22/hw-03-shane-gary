HTML+CSS & Marks+Channels

Each pie chart represents an item. The position on the axes (quantitative), size of the charts (quantitative), and proportions in the pie chart (quantitative for size, categorical for segments) are attributes. The mark are areas (items) and the channels are "position (both) on the graph, "area" of the entire pie chart, "color" of the pie segments, and "tilt" for the pie categories.

Each colored line on the points here represents an item, while the position vertically and horizontally represents attributes (both ordinal). The marks are points, which are used as items, and the channels are "color" of the points & lines and "position (both)" on the chart.

This chart displays items as nodes, and the attributes would be their category of color (categorical) and their name such as "Theme", "Idea", etc.(categorical). The marks are lines used as links (connection), between nodes, and the channel is position.

This chart displays items (A, B, C, D) and the attributes are the the segments themself (categorical) which each have quantitative measurements. The marks are lines used as items, and the channels are color "color" of the segments, "position (both)" on the x and y axes.
